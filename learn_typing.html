Intro

Typing requires some basic things, such as posture, finger placement, hand-eye coordination, and etc.


Posture

Posture means sitting up straight in your chair, not leaning or anything - that affects your typing speed!


Finger placement

Your fingers should be in the correct typing placement whenever you're at the keyboard (shown below) (for a QWERTY keyboard)


Do NOT do any other typing placement with your fingers, such as hunting and pecking!

Hand-eye coordination

Hand-eye coordination is essential for typing, because you need to be able to see the screen while typing WITHOUT looking down at your keyboard. This is because looking down at your keyboard not only slows down your typing speed, but it also affects your fingering and your accuracy takes a dip.


Now that you're in the setup, put your hands in the typing position and feel around, get a good grip.

Once you're done with that, start practicing a few basic typing texts!

The Space Bar

The space bar is an important part of typing. It the the thing that seperates the words to improve readability. In the olden times, there was no space bar and words had no separation! Just imagine how hard that would be to read!

When you're typing, always use the space bar to seperate your words. To do this, you can type words slowly to make sure you always press the space bar. Then, slowly increase your speed so your typing speed is good and your accuracy is perfect!

There's no practice for this lesson - just remember to use the space bar while typing to seperate words



Home Row

This is the home row in a standard QWERTY keyboard. It is where your fingers go automatically when you are not typing.

The fingering of the home row goes like this:

  • Left pinky: A
  • Left ring finger: S
  • Left middle finger: D
  • Left index finger: F
  • Right index finger: J
  • Right middle finger: K
  • Right ring finger: L
  • Right pinky: ;
  • Both thumbs will always be on the space bar

Once you've gotten your fingers in that position, start practicing!

Numbers and symbols

Numbers and symbols are also important in typing - they allow you to type distances, formulas, equations/expressions, and etc! They are also used for punctuation, like !, ., the comma(,), ?, /, and etc.

Numbers

The row above the top row is the numbers row.

Fingering of the numbers row goes like this:

  • Left pinky: 1
  • Left ring finger: 2
  • Left middle finger: 3
  • Left index finger: 4 and 5
  • Right index finger: 6 and 7
  • Right middle finger: 8
  • Right ring finger: 9
  • Right pinky: 0
  • Both thumbs will always be on the space bar

You can also type numbers from the keypad, if you have that.

Symbols

Symbols are usually part of letter rows and number rows.


These are only a few symbols on the typing keyboard.

This is the fingering of the symbols IN THE NUMBERS ROW:

  • Left pinky: !
  • Left ring finger: @
  • Left middle finger: #
  • Left index finger: $ and %
  • Right index finger: ^ and &
  • Right middle finger: *
  • Right ring finger: (
  • Right pinky: ), -, _, =, and +
  • Both thumbs will always be on the space bar

This is the fingering of the symbols IN THE LETTERS ROW

  • Right middle finger: <
  • Right ring finger: >
  • Right pinky: /, [, ], {, }
  • Both thumbs will always be on the space bar

Modifier keys

Here's the lesson about modifier keys, e.g. Caps Lock/Num Lock, Shift key, Ctrl key, Alt/Option key, etc.

You mostly type with modifier keys when you're pressing another key at the same time, because these keys do nothing while pressed alone. They are also mostly pressed with your pinkies (depending on which side the key is, the corresponding pinky types that) because pinkies are the closest fingers to modifiers.

Here are some of the functions:

  • Caps Lock: Types any letter in caps
  • Num Lock: Turns some parts of the keyboard numeric
  • Shift: Turns a letter/symbol to its uppercase
  • Ctrl: used for controls in editors and computers
  • Alt/option: it is also mostly for controls
  • Esc: Escapes out of text editor so you can't type there anymore

Note: These are only a few modifier keys

Other keys

There are other parts of the keyboard too. For example, the ` key, which is used in typing sometimes, the Tab key (which is like space but it indents your text, for formal essays and writing), the Backspace/Delete key, which is used to remove the most recent character typed, and Enter, which is used to create a new line within typing editors. There are more, of course.

There's no practice for this, because these keys are either too less used or too commonly used that you probably already know how to use them!
